Thinking About

Cultural robustness as a first-class property of vision models

AfriVTON-Bench was partly an attempt to make cultural robustness measurable rather than a vague aspiration. But "robustness to African textile patterns" is still one slice. What does a general framework look like, one that pins down measurable failures across high-frequency print structure, non-Western drape, skin-tone range, and culturally-specific garment grammar? And what training-data interventions actually move the needle versus dressing up the same distribution in new architecture?

Multilingual NLP beyond translation parity

Most "Nigerian language NLP" benchmarks evaluate translation or basic classification. But the retrieval gap matters more for end-use: people search for information, not translation. The BGE-M3 work nudged this, but the harder problem is building evaluation datasets that reflect how Nigerians actually query, code-mixed, register-shifting, domain-specific. Wondering what the right evaluation protocol looks like.

Clinical ML that survives deployment

A year of clinical practice has changed how I read papers. Models that report 95% on a curated benchmark often won't survive the first week in a real clinic, labels are noisy, imaging modalities shift, distribution shifts are constant. The few-shot blood smear work was a small attempt at this. The bigger question: what does the deployed-clinical-AI evaluation stack look like, separate from the publication-grade evaluation stack?

The path question, engineering, research, or clinic

I currently work across applied ML research, production-adjacent engineering, and clinical veterinary medicine. The three sharpen each other in unexpected ways, but each demands different long-term commitments. PhD route, applied research lab, or veterinary-AI specialization? Sitting with the question rather than rushing the answer.
