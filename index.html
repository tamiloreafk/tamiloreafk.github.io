01 /About Me

Research at the Intersection of Language, Vision, and Healthcare

I'm an applied machine learning researcher and a licensed veterinary doctor. My research sits where multilingual NLP, computer vision, and AI for healthcare meet, especially when those tools have to work for communities and contexts that mainstream models weren't trained for.

With ML Collective, I co-lead AfriVTON-Bench, a benchmark exposing how virtual try-on models trained on Western fashion corpora fail on African textile patterns and garment structures. The paper was accepted for oral presentation at the Women in Machine Learning (WiML) Workshop at ICML 2026. I also led the BGE-M3 multilingual embedding work for Nigerian low-resource languages, published at the 9th Widening NLP Workshop and awarded Best Poster at Deep Learning Indaba 2025.

Clinically, I serve as a Veterinary Doctor under the Nigerian NYSC scheme, handling diagnosis, surgery, vaccination, and zoonotic disease surveillance for small animals and livestock. The cross-discipline grounds the research: I think about model robustness, dataset gaps, and deployment constraints with the same caution I bring to a clinical case.

04 /Publications

Research

Peer-reviewed work on cultural robustness in generative vision models and multilingual retrieval for African languages.

Accepted · Oral · WiML Workshop @ ICML 2026

AfriVTON-Bench: Evaluating the Robustness of Virtual Try-On Models to African Textile Patterns and Garment Structures

Adekoya, T. O. & Oni, S. A. · 2026 · Seoul, South Korea

Benchmark exposing distribution-shift failures in VTON systems trained on Western fashion corpora. 111 African garment images across 16 categories, 7 countries, 5 sub-regions, paired with 15 diverse person model images for 1,012 evaluation pairs. Audited VITON-HD and DressCode for representation gaps. Surfaces three systematic failure modes, pattern dissolution, drape collapse, and tonal drift, across FASHN-VTON and LEFFA, pointing to the shared training distribution as root cause.

Computer Vision Generative Models Cultural Bias Benchmarking
Published · 9th Widening NLP Workshop, Suzhou · ACL 2025

Improving BGE-M3 Multilingual Dense Embeddings for Nigerian Low-Resource Languages

Omotoso, A., Shopeju, H., Joshua, A., & Oni, S. · 2025 · pp. 224–229

Fine-tuned BGE-M3 dense embedding model on three Nigerian low-resource languages for information retrieval over local document corpora, closing the multilingual coverage gap in standard retrieval encoders. Achieved a 20% improvement in Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) over the baseline across the three target languages.

Multilingual NLP Dense Retrieval Low-Resource Languages Best Poster, DLI 2025
